Resilient Peer-to-Peer Money

Bitcoin II (BC2) was created in 2018 to fulfill the original promise of Bitcoin as "A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System." It enables instant, near-zero cost payments directly between individuals without relying on a central authority.

Its core mission is to maintain a truly decentralized network. By using the X11 hashing algorithm, Bitcoin II is designed to be resistant to the specialized ASIC hardware that has centralized mining power on other networks. This ensures that anyone with consumer-grade computer hardware can participate in securing the network.

The Bitcoin II Advantage

ASIC Resistant Mining

The X11 algorithm allows for profitable mining with GPUs, preventing centralization and keeping the network secure and fair for everyone.

Faster Transactions

With a block time of approximately 2.5 minutes, BC2 transactions confirm 4x faster than Bitcoin (BTC).

Energy Efficient

Compared to SHA-256, the X11 algorithm requires less electricity to mine, resulting in a smaller environmental footprint.

Open Source

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Bitcoin II different from Bitcoin (BTC)?

The main difference is the mining algorithm. Bitcoin (BTC) uses SHA-256, which is now dominated by large, corporate-owned ASIC mining farms. Bitcoin II uses X11, an algorithm designed to be ASIC-resistant, which allows ordinary users to mine with their computer's GPU. This leads to a more decentralized and secure network, preventing a few large players from controlling the issuance of new coins.

Why use the X11 algorithm?

X11 is a chained hashing algorithm that uses a sequence of eleven different hashing functions. This complexity makes it significantly harder and more expensive to create specialized ASIC miners for it. The benefits are a more decentralized network, improved security through cryptographic depth, and lower energy consumption for GPU miners compared to SHA-256.

How can I start mining BC2?

You can mine Bitcoin II with a compatible GPU. To get started, you will need to download the core wallet, configure your mining software, and join a mining pool. You can find a list of active mining pools in our resources section.
